    What is EDUISSED?
    EDUISSED is the abbreviation of "Comprehensive Socio-Emotional Education in Digitalized Sexuality". It is an organization that aims to assist in the acquisition of socio-emotional skills in sexuality for young people.

    What is SEXi by EDUISSED?
    SEXi is a platform created by the EDUISSED team, which aims to support young people by providing them with tools that allow them to learn high-quality sexual education, as well as socio-emotional skills using augmented reality, infographics, games, and AI.
    Is the information provided by SEXi reliable?
    Yes, at EDUISSED, we care about the learning of our users; therefore, we provide information from reliable sources that you can consult. The topics and subtopics we present are based on the document "International Technical Guidelines on Sexuality Education" proposed by UNESCO in 2018. By clicking here, you can check all our sources of information.

    What is a male condom and what is it for?
    The male condom is a latex sheath that is placed on the erect penis before sexual intercourse and that covers it completely. How does it work? It prevents sperm from entering the vagina during sexual intercourse, as it acts as a barrier and thus prevents pregnancy.
    What is a female condom for and how is it used?
    The female condom is a barrier contraceptive method used to prevent pregnancy and protect against sexually transmitted infections (STIs). It is inserted into the vagina before sexual activity. It has a flexible ring at each end: one is placed in the back of the vagina and the other outside to cover the external genitals. This creates a barrier that prevents sperm from entering the vagina and reduces skin-to-skin contact, thus reducing the risk of STI transmission.
    What are mouth barriers and what are they used for?
    Mouth barriers are latex or polyurethane films used between the mouth and the vagina or anus during oral sex.
    What are the types of contraceptive methods that exist?
    There are several types of contraceptive methods available, each with its own characteristics and level of effectiveness. Some of the most common types include:
    • Hormonal:
      Pills, patches, injections, implants, and rings that release hormones to prevent ovulation.
    • Barrier methods:
      Condoms (male and female), diaphragm, cervical cap, and sponge, which create a physical barrier against sperm.
    • Intrauterine devices (IUDs):
      Copper or hormonal, they are placed in the uterus to prevent pregnancy.
    • Natural fertility methods:
      Monitoring the menstrual cycle to identify fertile days and avoid sexual intercourse during that time.
    • Surgical sterilization:
      Vasectomy for men and tubal ligation for women, permanent options.
    • Emergency contraceptives:
      Morning-after pill, to prevent pregnancy after unprotected sex or contraceptive failure.

What is SEXi?

Currently, teaching and learning strategies developed by the educational system through teachers are often repetitive and boring for students, which causes them to lose interest in performing activities and, as a consequence, the expected learning and understanding of the topics are not achieved.

That is why we, EDUISSED (Integral Socioemotional Education in Digitalized Sexuality), decided to design SEXi (Integral Sexuality), a technopedagogical tool, specifically a website, which aims to contribute to the acquisition of socioemotional skills in sexuality. What makes it different and more attractive is that it includes augmented reality to facilitate learning and, at the same time, make it more didactic and interesting for students.

It is important to use interactive methodologies that stimulate active participation, and not just transmit information, but also acquire socioemotional skills in sexuality.

Our work is based mainly on the information and topics provided by UNESCO, so it is recommended to review the links and references.
